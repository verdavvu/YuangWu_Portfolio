The Living Cultural Commons

Sixth Ward, Houston, Texas · ARCH 201 · Fall 2025 · Prof. Andrew Colopy

This residential project envisions housing as a quietly transformative landscape, encouraging civic engagement and integrating art into daily life. Interwoven volumes and alternating curves rise toward a central tower, while a sweeping roof recalls waves and sails, guiding movement inward toward shared cultural space.

The building hosts 32 homes, a public art gallery, and an artist studio that mediates between living and making. Carved passages, courtyards, and lightwells offer moments of stillness, framing daily life as an unfolding artistic experience.

At ground level, four staircases and a central elevator core organize circulation. Parking and bike storage support everyday mobility, while two interior courtyards bring light and air into the plan.

The inhabitable curved roof creates double-height residential spaces and a top-floor shared lounge, gym, and work area overlooking the courtyards.

Humanized Design in Commercial Center

MixC Commercial Center, Shanghai · Nihon Sekkei · Summer 2025

This project focused on enhancing human-centered design for vulnerable populations within a major commercial mall renovation. My work concentrated on interventions in the mother-baby room and underground light hall.

Design additions included baby care facilities, dedicated stroller space, portable furniture near elevators, improved signage, floor maps at each landing, and soft, luminous lighting to improve visibility and atmosphere.

Young Arts Center

Menil Campus, Houston · ARCH 102 · Spring 2025 · Prof. Scott Colman

Echoing the bungalow vernacular, the Young Arts Center features two slanted roofs anchoring the building within its neighborhood context. The project integrates art, education, and community through flowing circulation and varied sensory experiences.

A large flexible gathering space beneath the expansive roof serves as the central circulation spine, accommodating workshops, lectures, and public events. Carefully placed windows respond to structure, light, and climate.

Sectional design promotes visual connectivity through elevated walkways linking educational spaces while overlooking the central flex space and gardens.

Redesign of Fondren Library

Rice University · ARCH 101 · Fall 2024

The redesign transforms a static academic environment into a vibrant hub for collaborative learning. Circular reading rooms provide quiet focus while remaining visually connected to the larger space.

A revitalized central atrium improves circulation, orientation, and openness, functioning as the library’s social and spatial nucleus.
